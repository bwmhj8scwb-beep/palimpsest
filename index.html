Map key

Gold thread: where power sits, and how far decisions travel.

Moving dots: people on the move. Colors distinguish displacement/refugees, Jewish immigration, return from exile, settlement growth, and pilgrimage.

Map story markers: little dark chips move important essay ideas onto the places they happened.

Sacred places: glowing nodes show which holy sites matter most in each era.

Map notes: the small note on each map gives the big idea for that slide.

Names: labels are used where they clarify the period; modern identities are not pasted backward onto ancient people.

About this map & sources

This is a schematic guide, not a survey atlas: borders are washes of colour, the coastline is stylised, and population flows show direction and rough scale, not exact figures. It is built to be fair to a contested history, not to settle it — where one event carries two names (1948 above all), both are given.

Colours are pigments, not flags. Where scripture and archaeology diverge, the text says so. The present-day slide reflects events as of early 2026 (the October 2025 Gaza ceasefire, the “Yellow Line”, and UN Resolution 2803) and will need updating over time.

It draws on standard reference history rather than any single partisan source — works such as the Cambridge and Oxford histories of the region, and the framing of nature's legal personhood and contested naming used by historians and geographers of the Levant. Corrections are welcome.
